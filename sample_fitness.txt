Squats are a compound exercise that primarily targets the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. The exercise is performed using body weight or a barbell placed across the upper back. Proper form requires keeping the chest up and the lower back neutral throughout the movement. The squat begins from a standing position, descends until the hips are below the knees, and returns to standing.

Deadlifts are another fundamental compound movement that engages the entire posterior chain including the erector spinae, glutes, and hamstrings. The conventional deadlift uses a barbell and begins with the weight on the floor. The lifter hinges at the hips while maintaining a flat back, grips the bar with both hands, and stands up by extending the hips and knees simultaneously.

The bench press is an upper body exercise that targets the pectoralis major, anterior deltoids, and triceps brachii. It is performed lying on a flat bench while pressing a barbell from the chest to full arm extension. The leg press machine provides an alternative to squats for those with lower back issues, isolating the quadriceps while reducing spinal loading.

For core stability, planks and Russian twists are recommended. Planks engage the rectus abdominis and transverse abdominis without equipment. Russian twists additionally target the obliques and can be weighted with a medicine ball for progression.
